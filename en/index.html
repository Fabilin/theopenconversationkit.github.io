Tock, an open conversational AI platform.

Complete solution to build conversational agents aka bots.

Not depending on 3rd-party APIs. Ownership of models and data.

Sources available on GitHub. Promoted by TOSIT (The Open Source I Trust).

In production for years over numerous channels (+).

Language Understanding

Complete and autonomous NLU solution leveraging opensource libs, such as OpenNLP, Stanford, Duckling and +.

Ubiquitous Assistants

Web, mobile, social networks, smart speakers... Create your bot once, connect progressively to all your channels as needed.

Tock Studio

Simple graphical interfaces to build stories and models, manage multilingual and multichannel bots, monitor logs and analytics.

Framework & API

Program complex stories using Kotlin or Node.js provided DSLs, or integrate any other language through the Bot API.

Deploy anywhere

Try Tock online, deploy in minutes using Docker running configurations, or setup your own platform to the Cloud, On-Premise, even embedded.

Active community.

More and more teams and companies trust in Tock and open conversational platforms.

AlloCovid

Built around a Tock bot, the French AlloCovid service informs and guides population about the Covid-19. Designed by French experts, partners and volunteers, it is available by phone, on the Web and WhatsApp.

Find out more by visiting www.allocovid.com. The bot sources are available here.

Best Open Source Strategy 2019

In 2019 the Acteurs du Libre award for the Best Open Source Strategy is given to SNCF for creating and sharing open platforms such as Tock (e.Voyageurs) and Navitia (Kisio Digital).

Award ceremony: December 10th at the Grand Auditorium of Paris Open Source Summit (photos).

Paris Open Source Summit

The creators of Tock and guests from SNCF, Enedis, EDF, Orange, SogetiLabs and TOSIT share experience and stakes in play with " Conversational AI & Open Source": open innovation, time to market, digital sovereignty, sustainability, governance...

The Paris Open Source Summit channel makes the conference available online (in French).

Hackathon for all!

SNCF prepares a two-day open hackathon on Conversational AI using its Tock platform. Everyone can register and bring coding skills and use cases, such as travel, accessibility, entertainment... May the best team wins!

Rendez-vous November 21-22 at Espace 574 (St Denis) and the Hack & Bot Website.
